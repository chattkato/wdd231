Section 01

Site Name

OrbitBase

The name OrbitBase is formed by combining two defining concepts: orbit — the path a satellite follows around a celestial body — and base, which signals both a base of knowledge and a database of catalog entries. Together they describe a reference hub where visitors can explore, search, and learn about satellites and space missions.

The name is short, technically relevant, and easy to remember. It works as a standalone brand without requiring further explanation, and reads well as a domain name.

Optional domain: orbitbase.space
Section 02

Site Purpose

OrbitBase is a three-page catalog website that allows visitors to browse and explore a curated directory of active and historical satellites and space missions. The site fetches structured mission data from a local JSON file and displays each entry with key details including orbit type, operator, launch date, altitude, mass, and mission purpose.

A filterable and searchable catalog page allows users to narrow results by orbit class, mission type, and operational status. A modal dialog provides a full mission profile on demand without leaving the page. A contact and subscription form lets space enthusiasts stay connected with site updates. Local storage is used to remember the visitor's last filter preferences across sessions.

The site targets students, hobbyists, satellite industry professionals, and anyone curious about what is currently orbiting Earth and beyond.

Section 03

Scenarios

The following questions represent real visitors interacting with OrbitBase. They define the target audience and drive the content, features, and structure of each page.

  • I want to know which satellites are currently in low Earth orbit — how many are there and who operates them?
  • I work in the telecom industry and need to quickly identify communication satellites by coverage region and operator.
  • I am a student researching space missions for a class project — where can I find a list of missions with launch dates and purposes?
  • I heard about a new satellite launch recently — can I find it in a catalog and see its orbital altitude and mission data?
Section 04

Color Scheme

The palette is drawn from the visual language of deep space: near-black backgrounds evoking the void, electric blue for active interface elements, and violet for secondary highlights referencing the spectral color of nebulae imagery. This document uses these colors exclusively.

Deep Space#050A18

Primary page background on all three site pages.

Surface Navy#0D1526

Electric Sky#4FC3F7

Primary accent: links, CTAs, active nav, badges, stat numbers.

Nebula Violet#B388FF

Secondary accent: type labels, orbit tags, scenario borders.

Starlight#E8EDF5

Body text, paragraph content, card descriptions.

Dust Haze#7A90B0

Muted text: metadata, captions, placeholders, inactive nav.

Section 06

Page Overview

The project consists of three primary pages plus a form action page and an attributions page.

File Page Name Purpose
index.html Home Hero, animated orbit diagram, stats counter, 6 featured satellite cards, orbit types explainer.
catalog.html Satellite Catalog All 20 satellites as cards. Filter by orbit, type, status; search; sort by name, date, altitude, or mass. Modal on click.
contact.html Contact & Subscribe Contact form with validation, area of interest selector, subscribe checkbox. Displays submitted data on the same page.
attributions.html Attributions Credits for images, data sources, and fonts. Linked from footer. Not counted toward the 3-page requirement.
