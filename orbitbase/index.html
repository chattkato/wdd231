Orbital Classes

How Satellites Are Organised by Altitude

LEO

Low Earth Orbit

160 – 2,000 km altitude. Used for Earth observation, communications constellations, and crewed stations. Short orbital periods of 90–120 minutes.

MEO

Medium Earth Orbit

2,000 – 35,786 km altitude. The domain of navigation satellites like GPS and Galileo. Orbital periods range from 2 to 24 hours.

GEO

Geostationary Orbit

35,786 km altitude. Satellites appear stationary over a fixed point on Earth — ideal for weather monitoring and broadcasting.

SSO

Sun-Synchronous Orbit

Polar orbit designed so the satellite always passes over the same area at the same local solar time — essential for Earth observation consistency.
